<h3>Game specification</h3><p></p><h4>1. Components</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Game boards (Worlds):</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Count:</strong> 6 distinct boards, labeled World 1–6.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Structure:</strong> Each world is a path or network of spaces. Some spaces are:</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Movement spaces:</strong> Move within the world.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Inter‑world spaces:</strong> Teleport to another world.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Special spaces:</strong> Store, challenge spots, bonus/penalty spaces.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Game cards:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Types:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Movement cards:</strong> Move to a specified space or world.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Challenge cards:</strong> Require a task/mini‑game/condition; success/failure affects money, gems, or movement.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Economy cards:</strong> Add/remove money and/or gems.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Store access cards (optional):</strong> Allow or modify access to the Store.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Dice:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Standard:</strong> At least one fair 6‑sided die.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Use:</strong> Determine starting world and movement within worlds (unless overridden by cards or powerups).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Currency and items:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Money:</strong> Integer or decimal values, tracked on paper.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Gems:</strong> Discrete units, may have types or colors (optional) with different values.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Powerups:</strong> Player‑defined abilities purchased at the Store.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Tracking tools:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Paper and pencil:</strong> Track:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Player money and gems</p><p></p></li><li><p>Powerups owned</p><p></p></li><li><p>Turn count (if needed)</p><p></p></li><li><p>Final ValuEquation calculations</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Players:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Count:</strong> 2 or more players; no maximum defined, limited by physical space and components.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h4>2. Pre‑game setup</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>2.1 Choose or create world layouts</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>World definition:</strong> Players agree on:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Number of spaces in each world</p><p></p></li><li><p>Connections between spaces</p><p></p></li><li><p>Which spaces lead to other worlds</p><p></p></li><li><p>Locations of Stores and special spaces</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>All world layouts must be visible to all players.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>2.2 Cooperative powerup design</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Step 1 – Brainstorm powerups:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Players propose candidate powerups (e.g., extra movement, reroll, discount, gem multiplier, card manipulation, protection, etc.).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Step 2 – Define each powerup:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Name</strong></p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Effect</strong> (what it does, when it is used, how often)</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Limitations</strong> (per turn, per game, conditions)</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Cost</strong> in money and/or gems</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Step 3 – Group agreement:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>All players must agree that:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Each powerup is understandable.</p><p></p></li><li><p>No powerup grants infinite or unbounded resources.</p><p></p></li><li><p>No powerup makes a player effectively uncatchable or uninteractive.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>Powerups and costs are written down where everyone can see.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>2.3 Game length and end condition</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Before the game starts, players agree on one of:</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Fixed turn limit:</strong> e.g., 20, 30, or 50 full rounds.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Time limit:</strong> e.g., 60 minutes, then complete the current round.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Event‑based end:</strong> e.g., when a total of N gems have been collected across all players, or when every player has visited at least 3 different worlds.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>This is written down and not changed mid‑game.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>2.4 ValuEquation definition</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>The ValuEquation is a single scoring function applied at the end to each player.</p><p></p></li><li><p>The equation must be:</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Known to all players</strong> before the game starts.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Fixed</strong> for the entire game (no mid‑game changes).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>It may use:</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Money:</strong> M</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Number of gems:</strong> G</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Gem types (optional):</strong> G1,G2,…</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Powerup count:</strong> P</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Timing variables:</strong> such as early vs late purchases</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Worlds visited:</strong> e.g., W = number of different worlds entered</p><p></p></li><li><p>Other agreed parameters (e.g., challenges completed C).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>Allowed operations:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Addition, subtraction, multiplication, division</p><p></p></li><li><p>Exponents (with reasonable bounds)</p><p></p></li><li><p>Fractions, decimals, percentages</p><p></p></li><li><p>Parameters (constants chosen by players pre‑game)</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Example style (not required):</strong></p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><p>V=(M+10⋅G)⋅(1+0.05⋅P)+50⋅W−3⋅Cfailed</p><ul><li><p>The exact form is up to the group, but must be:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Computable with a calculator</p><p></p></li><li><p>Finite (no infinities or undefined operations)</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h4>3. Setup per player</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>3.1 Starting state</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Money:</strong> M=0</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Gems:</strong> G=0 and/or all gem types = 0</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Powerups:</strong> none</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Position:</strong> off‑board initially</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>3.2 Assign starting worlds</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Each player rolls the die once.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Their starting world is the world with the same number as the rolled value (1–6).</p><p></p></li><li><p>If custom rules exist for rolling 6 (e.g., choose any world), they must be agreed before the game.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>3.3 Place players</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Each world defines a starting space.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Each player places their token on the starting space of their assigned world.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h4>4. Turn structure</h4><p></p><p>On a player’s turn, the sequence is:</p><p></p><ol><li><p><strong>Start‑of‑turn effects</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Apply persistent powerups or status effects</strong> that trigger automatically at the start of turn, if any.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Movement phase</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Roll the die</strong> (unless a card or powerup overrides this).</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Move</strong> the player’s token forward or along paths according to:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>The die result</p><p></p></li><li><p>The structure of the world</p><p></p></li><li><p>Any movement rules (loops, branches, etc.)</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Space resolution</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>After movement, resolve the effect of the space landed on:</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Card space:</strong> Draw the top card from the relevant deck and follow its instructions.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Store space:</strong> Player may perform Store actions (see Store section).</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Inter‑world space:</strong> Transfer the player’s token to the specified world and entry space.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Challenge space:</strong> Perform the challenge; update money/gems accordingly.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Special space:</strong> Any custom effect agreed beforehand.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Card resolution (if any)</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>When a card is drawn:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Read it aloud.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Apply movement, money changes, gem changes, or extra actions in the order defined by the card.</p><p></p></li><li><p>If the card sends you to another space/world, resolve that space as well (unless the group rules limit chain reactions—see safety section later).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Store phase (if applicable)</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>If the player is on a Store space and is allowed to interact:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>The player may buy any number of powerups and/or gems, as long as:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>They can pay the cost.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Purchases follow any per‑turn or per‑game limits on powerups.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>All transactions are recorded.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>End‑of‑turn effects</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Apply any effects that trigger at the end of the turn.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Check if the end condition of the game has been reached.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Play passes to the next player.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ol><p></p><h4>5. Store and economy</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>5.1 Store inventory</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Items available:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Player‑defined <strong>powerups</strong> (see above).</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Gems</strong> at a group‑defined price (may be fixed or scaling).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>Optional: Other items (temporary boosts, one‑time cards) if agreed.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>5.2 Purchases</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Payment:</strong> Money and/or gems as defined by the powerup cost.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Ownership:</strong> Powerups belong to the player once bought and cannot be sold back unless a pre‑defined rule allows it.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Usage:</strong> Each powerup defines when and how it is used (automatic, once per turn, once per game, reaction to other events, etc.).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>5.3 Economic flow</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Money and gems are gained or lost through:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Cards</p><p></p></li><li><p>Challenges</p><p></p></li><li><p>Special spaces</p><p></p></li><li><p>Possibly trades between players, if allowed by pre‑game rules</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h4>6. End of game and scoring</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>6.1 Triggering the end</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>When the pre‑defined end condition is met:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>If it is a turn limit, finish the current round so all players have equal turns.</p><p></p></li><li><p>If event‑based, end immediately or at the end of the current round (decided pre‑game).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>6.2 Compute ValuEquation</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>For each player:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Collect all required values (money, gems, worlds visited, powerups, challenges, etc.).</p><p></p></li><li><p>Plug them into the ValuEquation.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Use a calculator if needed.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Record each player’s final value V.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>6.3 Determine winners</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Only players whose V is within the range 550≤V≤100000 are eligible to win.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Among them:</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Primary:</strong> The player(s) with the highest V win.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Ties:</strong> If multiple players share the same highest V within the allowed range, all of them win.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>If no player’s value is in the allowed range, there is <strong>no winner</strong>.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h3>Preventing infinite loops, exploits, and system breaking</h3><p></p><h4>1. Preventing infinite loops</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>1.1 Movement and board loops</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Rule:</strong> No card or powerup may force the same exact sequence of spaces to repeat endlessly without player choice.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Practical constraints:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Limit chain reactions:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Rule:</strong> A single turn may not contain more than a fixed number of additional moves caused by cards/powerups (e.g., at most 3 extra movements triggered by effects).</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Teleport controls:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>If a space teleports you to another space that teleports you back, add a rule:</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Rule:</strong> If you enter the same space twice in one turn due to movement effects, you ignore any further automatic movement from that space for the rest of the turn.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>1.2 Powerup activation loops</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Rule:</strong> No powerup may trigger itself directly or indirectly without a cost or limit.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Design constraints:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Per‑turn use limit:</strong> e.g., “This powerup can be used at most once per turn.”</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Per‑game limit:</strong> e.g., “Use this powerup up to 3 times per game.”</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Cost to activate:</strong> Some powerups may require money or gems to activate; they can’t generate more of the same resource needed to activate them in a way that leads to infinite gain.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>1.3 Economic loops</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Rule:</strong> You cannot create a combination of powerups/cards that produces net positive money or gems with no risk and no limit.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Check when designing:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>If a powerup converts money → gems and another converts gems → money, ensure the exchange rates cause a <strong>loss</strong> or at best break even over a cycle, not profit.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h4>2. Preventing exploits and degenerate strategies</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>2.1 Overpowered powerups</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Playtest rule:</strong> Before starting, simulate a few turns in your head:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Ask: “If someone gets this powerup early, can anyone else realistically catch up?”</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Balance rule:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>If a powerup:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Gives huge extra money every turn</p><p></p></li><li><p>Allows infinite rerolls until perfect results</p><p></p></li><li><p>Lets you ignore almost all negative effects<br>Then either:</p><p></p></li><li><p>Increase its cost significantly, or</p><p></p></li><li><p>Add strict usage limits, or</p><p></p></li><li><p>Remove it.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>2.2 Farming single “best” action</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>If the game ends up with one clearly best way to gain money/gems (e.g., one challenge that is always easy, or a space that always gives big rewards):</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Rule:</strong> Limit how often the same benefit can be gained:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>“You cannot intentionally land on this space more than once every 3 turns.”</p><p></p></li><li><p>“This challenge’s reward is reduced each time the same player completes it.”</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>2.3 Breaking the ValuEquation</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>When creating the ValuEquation, prevent:</p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Division by small or zeroish values:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Don’t include divisions like 1000gems that explode when gems are small.</p><p></p></li><li><p>If you must divide by something, ensure it’s always at least 1 or has a minimum.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Huge exponents:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Exponents like M5 or 2G can grow out of control.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Use small exponents (like 2) and/or small coefficients.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Unbounded multipliers:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Things like “10,000 times number of powerups” can make one mechanic dominate everything.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>Add a <strong>sanity check rule</strong>:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>After designing the equation, test it with:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Very low values (everything near 0)</p><p></p></li><li><p>Very high but plausible values</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>Confirm the results still fall near the desired range (up to around 100000) for realistic play.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h4>3. Protecting the randomness and fairness</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>3.1 Dice fairness</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Visible rolling:</strong> All dice must be rolled where everyone can see.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Flat surface:</strong> Dice are rolled on a clear, stable surface.</p><p></p></li><li><p><strong>No re‑rolling unless allowed:</strong> Players cannot re‑roll unless a card or powerup explicitly permits it.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>3.2 Card fairness</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Shuffling:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Shuffle all decks thoroughly before the game and whenever they are reshuffled.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>No deck peeking:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>No looking at upcoming cards unless a powerup/card says so.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Discard rules:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Use a discard pile; reshuffle it into a new deck when the main deck runs out.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>3.3 Information symmetry</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Open information:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Money, gems, owned powerups, and position are visible to all players.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>Known rules:</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Powerup definitions, Store prices, and the ValuEquation are all written where players can see them.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h4>4. Anti‑cheating rules</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>4.1 Rule clarity</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Write all group decisions:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Powerups and costs</p><p></p></li><li><p>End condition</p><p></p></li><li><p>ValuEquation</p><p></p></li><li><p>Any house rules</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>If something is ambiguous during the game, pause and agree on a resolution before proceeding.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>4.2 Dispute resolution</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>Rule:</strong> If a disagreement occurs, use one of:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Majority vote</p><p></p></li><li><p>Neutral “judge” player (rotating role)</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>Once a ruling is made for a situation, apply it consistently for the rest of the game.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>4.3 No retroactive changes</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Players cannot:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Change previously recorded money/gems</p><p></p></li><li><p>Rewrite the ValuEquation mid‑game</p><p></p></li><li><p>Redefine powerups after the game starts</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p><h4>5. Keeping the system stable and fun</h4><p></p><ul><li><p><strong>5.1 Complexity boundaries</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>The ValuEquation should be complex, but:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>It must be finished before the game starts.</p><p></p></li><li><p>It should be understandable when reading it, even if not easily solved mentally.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>If it gets too long, you can:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Break it into parts (e.g., base score + bonus score).</p><p></p></li><li><p>Still keep it as a single formula but formatted clearly.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>5.2 Turn length and flow</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>To avoid super‑long turns:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>Limit how many powerups can be activated in one turn.</p><p></p></li><li><p>Limit chain reactions from cards.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p>This keeps the game flowing so everyone stays engaged.</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li><li><p><strong>5.3 “Fun first” override</strong></p><p></p><ul><li><p>Meta‑rule the group can adopt:</p><p></p><ul><li><p>“If we discover a rule or combo that completely kills the fun or makes the game trivial, we can all agree to change or ban it after the game, and optionally mid‑game if everyone unanimously agrees.”</p><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><p></p></li></ul><hr><hr><hr><hr><hr><p></p>
